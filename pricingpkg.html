"Vision Statement of the P’egp’íg’lha Clan"

We are the P’egp’íg’lha of the St’át’imc Nation. The Creator placed us here and that connection to our land can never be broken...

Origin of P'egp'ig'lha Data Sovereignty Commission and Board of Directors

In September of 2023, it was brought to Chief and Councils' attention through a letter from a concerned community member at the P'egp'iglha Assembly that action needed to be taken to ensure P'egp'ig'lha Data Sovereignty and P'egp'ig'lha Title and Rights were upheld, and the decision to found the P'egp'ig'lha Data Sovereignty Commission with a Board of Directors was made.

Our Mandate

  • To Provide P'egp'ig'lha with Advanced Off-grid Infrastructure Enabling Cloud-free Data Storage and Sovereignty
  • All data housed in the system will be Located on P'egp'ig'lha Traditional Territory and All Operations to be carried out by P'egp'ig'lha Personnel
  • Identify Community Specializations and Build Capacity Accordingly
  • Identify Key Components and Community Need to Enact P'egp'ig'lha Cyber-Security Branch under the authority of T'it'q'et
  • Be the Governing Body for Digital Data Sovereignty and Independence in Cultural, Familial, and Heritage and Community Intellectual Property Storage
  • Provide Community with Computer Technology and Computer Science Education at every appropriate opportunity
  • Provide Strategic Support to St'at'imc Nation Communities Who Are Also Striving for Data Independence

ST’AT’IMC SELF-GOVERNANCE, SELF-DETERMINATION AND ENERGY INDEPENDENCE

St’at’imc self-governance and self-determination is something that the St’at’imc have been working on since first contact...

Summary: The text advocates for the St’at’imc Nation to consider nuclear atomic energy as a means to achieve self-reliance, self-governance, and energy independence. By creating a St’at’imc Atomic Energy Commission and adopting a 7-generation approach, the community aims to build safer and more sustainable energy systems. The author highlights the benefits of nuclear power, including higher energy output and a smaller environmental footprint compared to hydro facilities. They acknowledge the concerns about nuclear meltdowns but propose advanced designs to mitigate such risks. Job creation and economic growth are also emphasized as potential benefits. The text envisions a brighter future where nuclear energy can lead to the removal of existing hydro infrastructure, the restoration of rivers, and the return of migrating salmon to their spawning grounds, contributing to the well-being of future generations.
